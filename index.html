Before you start
A few tips to get the most out of your training and develop critical ears faster
🎧
Use proper headphones
Always train with a good pair of over-ear headphones. Earbuds and laptop speakers color the sound too much and will train your ears incorrectly. Bluetooth is not preferable — wired is always more accurate. If you must use Bluetooth, make sure your headphones and phone both support LDAC for the highest quality wireless audio.
🔌
Use a decent DAC/dongle
Your phone's built-in audio output is often noisy and limited. A simple USB-C DAC dongle makes a huge difference — it gives you cleaner, more accurate audio so you can hear subtle EQ changes properly.
🎯
Stick with your production headphones
Train with the same headphones you use to mix and produce. This builds a deep understanding of how YOUR headphones color the sound — which is exactly what you need to make better mixing decisions faster.
📈
Be consistent, not intense
15–30 minutes of focused daily training beats hour-long sessions once a week. Your ears and brain need repeated exposure over time to build real frequency memory. Use the daily streak to stay on track.
🎵
Use your own music
Once you're comfortable with pink noise, switch to music you actually work with. Training on familiar tracks makes it much easier to notice EQ changes and builds practical ear memory for real sessions.
